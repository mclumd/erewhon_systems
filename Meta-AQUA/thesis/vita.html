VITA

Michael T. Cox was born on February 11, 1955 in Jacksonville, Florida. He attended Zephyrhills high School in Zephyrhills, Florida, where he graduated as valedictorian. After high school he received a nomination to the U.S. Air Force Academy in Colorado Springs, Colorado, where he attended for eight months. Upon returning to Florida, he attended Eckerd College in St. Petersburg, Florida for two years. After six years as a self-employed salesperson, he returned to college in the fall of 1982 as a NL Scholar. He graduated second in his undergraduate class at the Georgia Institute of Technology in December, 1986, receiving a Bachelor's of Science degree in Information and Computer Science with highest honors. He started a computer consulting company called microSpheres, completing subcontracts with businesses and the academic faculty of Georgia Tech and Duke University. He entered the Ph.D. program in the College of Computing at Georgia Tech in the fall of 1989, receiving a doctorate in Computer Science in March, 1996.

His interests in the fields of artificial intelligence and cognitive science include, case-based reasoning, memory (especially forgetting), failure-driven learning, explanation, metacognition, and introspection. He has been a member of the American Association for Artificial Intelligence since 1990 and a member of the Cognitive Science Society since 1991.
